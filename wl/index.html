Why delete a bunk?

Room for three.
Space for everything else.

Not every family needs four beds. If you are travelling with one, two or three kids, the fourth bunk usually ends up as a shelf for bags, toys and washing. The Quad Bunk Delete layout turns that wasted bed into purpose-built storage, so the kids' clothes, gear and sanity all have a home.

Everything else that makes the XT2311F-B4 a family favourite stays put. The private north-south island queen up front, the L-shape lounge the whole crew fits around, and the door-side kitchen that keeps you part of the campsite conversation while dinner is on.

*Copy note: confirm with production exactly what replaces the deleted bunk (cupboard, hanging robe or open bay) before publish.

Step inside

Every zone, working hard

Everyone gets their own spot

Parents up front, kids down back. The island queen sits north-south with walkway access on both sides, so nobody climbs over anyone at 2am. The three bunks each get their own drawers, and the space where the fourth bunk would sit works for gear instead of guests.

  • Caravan queen bed 1530 x 1880mm, north-south island orientation
  • Luxurious pillow top mattress with walkway access down each side
  • Three full-size bunks in the rear kids' zone
  • Deleted bunk bay converted to storage (fit-out TBC)
  • Lounge converts to an extra bed with in-fill cushion (sold separately)

Pack for the term, not the weekend

Family travel means family gear. This layout was already the storage king of the range. Deleting the fourth bunk adds another dedicated bay right where the kids' clutter lives.

  • 670L tunnel boot (700W x 430H x 2245D) via external doors
  • 650L under-bed storage (1100W x 450H x 1360D) via gas-lift bed
  • Under-bunk storage drawers plus built-in drawers per bunk (count TBC for 3-bunk config)
  • Mirrored wardrobes with hanging space each side of the queen
  • Extra storage bay in place of the fourth bunk (fit-out TBC)

The family hub

The L-shape lounge is where homework, card games and rainy day movies happen. The telescopic table adjusts to any height, swivels and slides in four directions, and drops down to make an extra bed when the kids bring a mate.

  • L-shape lounge with telescopic, adjustable table leg
  • Table swivels and slides in four directions for easy access
  • Converts to an extra bed with in-fill cushion (sold separately)
  • Fold-out footrest for comfortable lounging

Meals with a view

The straight door-side kitchen faces the awning, so whoever is cooking stays part of the campsite action. A 300mm full-height pantry swallows the big shop.

  • Straight door-side kitchen for outdoor visibility while cooking
  • 300mm full-height pantry
  • Kitchen overhead cabinets above the fridge and seating area
  • Six kitchen drawers and an under-sink cabinet

Freshen up, wash up

A proper rear bathroom with shower, vanity and toilet, plus a wall-mounted washing machine, because three kids generate washing at an industrial rate.

  • Medium rear bathroom with shower, vanity, storage and toilet
  • 3kg space-saving wall-mounted washing machine
  • Bathroom overhead and vanity cabinets
  • Laundry chest and three open shelves
